Time Dilation & Length Contraction
Time dilation is a concept in Albert Einstein's theory of relativity. Time dilation explains that time moves differently for a different obervers in the same inertial frame. Essentially time wil move slower depending on the observer moving close to relativistic speed to another observer. Take the famous explanation, one observer is standing on a platform while the other is on a moving train. Both have synchronized clocks and let us assume the train is moving close to the speed of light 0.99c. The clock on the moving train will move slower compared to the clock on the platform. Meaning that time is not absolute and depends on the frame of reference.

Now what is interesting with time dilation is that it can occur due to a presence of a gravitational field, meaning time passes slower in stronger gravitational fields. Let's say you have a twin. You are living on Earth while your twin lives on Mars. Due to Mars having a significantly weaker gravitational field compared to Earth, your twin will age slower compared to you.

Length Contraction is another concept in Albert Einstein's theory of relativity. Essentially, length contraction is when a moving objects length is determined to be shorter in a moving frame compared to its proper length in a rest frame. Think of it like this, a stationary observer is in a rocketship. A different observer is moving in rocketship going relativistic speed near the speed of light. The stationary observer views the moving observer and notices the length of rocketship has shrunk due to relativistic speed the observer is moving in one direction.

The Doppler Effect
The doppler effect is a phenomenon brought up by Albert Einstein's theory of relativity. It is defined as the increase or decrease in the frequency of a wave as the source and observer move towards or away from each other. When moving away an object will experience a "redshift". When moving closer an object will experence a "blueshift". A very interesting phenomenon. Where do we see this phenomenon?

NASA has captured images of several galaxies. You will be able to see their are some galaxies more red colored or blue colored compared to the rest.

All credit goes to NASA for this image:

Notice the galaxies that are more red in color, these galaxies are experiencing a redshift. The telescope is observing these galaxies far from our own. We all know that the universe is expanding. Some of these galaxies are moving away from the observer, hence the traveling light waves are becoming stretched out or larger in scale. Meaning the traveling light wave will be red in wavelength, hence a red shift when viewed by an observer.
